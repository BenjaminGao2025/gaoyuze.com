Private thoughts decay when they never meet form. They feel important in the moment, then blur into mood, intention, and unfinished self-explanation.

This site begins with a simple countermeasure: write the thought down, give it a date, and let it stand where future attention can return to it.

The goal is not performance. The goal is pressure. A public archive forces a mind to become more exact. It asks whether an idea can survive a paragraph, whether a conviction can become a sentence, and whether a sentence still feels true after sleep.

I want this place to become a record of apprenticeship. Reading should leave traces. Work should leave ledgers. Questions should become essays before they become identities.

The first rule is small enough to keep: publish evidence of thinking.
