Attention is often discussed as if it were a moral quality. Some people are disciplined; others are distracted. That story is too convenient.

A better question is operational: what did attention produce today?

If the answer is only a feeling of busyness, the day may have been consumed without being converted. If the answer is a paragraph, a note, a solved problem, a conversation remembered accurately, or a page of useful questions, attention has left a trace.

The trace matters because it can be compared. Comparison makes growth less mystical. A week of traces shows what kinds of work actually happened, not what the mind claimed was important while it was tired.

The ledger does not need to be severe. It only needs to be honest enough to interrupt drift.
